The Origins of DOGE

It all started with just a "dank" meme, and a collective dream. DOGE coin was the idea of two software engineers Billy Markus (of IBM) and Jackson Palmer (of Adobe). The newly create crypto currency started out as a joke, but drastically took the internet by storm after acknowledgement from the tech giant himself, Elon Musk. DOGE coin was created December 6th 2013, hitting a market cap of US$85,314,347,523 on May 5, 2021. The goal of Billy and Jackson for creating DOGE coin besides it being a joke, was to create a peer-to-peer digital currency that could reach a broader demographic than the raining king of crypto, Bitcoin. Upon their initial launch, dogecoin.com received over a million visits alone. Jackson Palmer is most credited for making their dream a reality by purchasing the dogecoin.com domain, and adding a splash screen, which featured the infamous DOGE coin logo and scattered Comic Sans text.

During his time on SNL, Elon joked about about having a role to spread memes pertaining to cryptocurrency.
